Beef Stew


Description:

Perfect for winter or wet, miserable days - a traditional braised beef stew with thick gravy and chunky veg, slow cooked for the perfect meal

Below we have two methods, the oven method if you have some time or a slow cooker method for an easier way!

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 Beef stock cubes (crumbled)
  • 1kg stewing steak, diced in large chunks
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Potatoes: chopped

Directions (oven):

  1. Preheat oven to 160C, 140C fan (gas mark 3)
  2. Put the onion, carrots and bay leaves into a casserole dish with the oil and butter. Add potatoes if you wish
  3. Allow to sit and soften for 5 minutes, then stir in 2 tbsp plain flour
  4. After the flour is fully stirred in, add 2tbsp tomato puree, the Worcestershire sauce and sprinkle over the 2 crumbled beef stock cubes
  5. Gradually stir in 600ml of hot (not boiling) water, then add the stewing beef and bring to a simmer
    Optional: Lightly coat the beef in flour, brown in a low-medium heat frying pan to lock in more flavour before adding to the dish
  6. Cover and put in the oven for 2:30hrs, uncover and cook for a further 30mins until the meat is tender

Directions (slow cooker):

This is a really easy method, takes roughly 5-10 minutes to prep and will be ready when you get home!

  1. Preheat slow cooker on high (around 5-10 minutes will be fine, keep the lid on!)
  2. Chop the onion and carrots, set aside
  3. Heat a frying pan on low-medium, brown the diced beef in batches(lightly coat in flour to thicken the flavour)
  4. Add 600ml boiling water put the two stock cubes, plus the Worcestershire sauce in and mix well
  5. Place the onions, carrots and beef into the slow cooker. Add the potatoes and pour the stock in, stir everything in and ensure the meat is covered
  6. Add the butter, puree and bay leaves and give it a stir
  7. Cook on high for 4 hours, or low for 8 hours.
    You'll want the meat to be tender and the sauce to thicken
  8. Leave the lid on for the entire cooking time, it does not need to be stirred.
    If the gravy is not thick enough when you're wanting to eat, mix one teaspoon cornflour with a tablespoon of room temperature water
    Transfer the sauce only to a saucepan and add the mix
    Ensure you mix well and bring it back to a boil until you reach the desired thickness. Readd it to the slow cooker, stir well and serve!
