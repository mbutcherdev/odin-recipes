Steak Pie

Description:

Nothing beats a homemade classic, the steak pie. Complete with a golden-brown flaky pastry and a rich beef filling, taking only 15 minutes to prep!

Ingredients:

  • Sunflower oil
  • 1kg braising steak, diced
  • 2 onions, roughly chopped
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • 3 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 2 beef stock cubes (600ml boiling water)
  • 375g ready-rolled puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten

Directions:

Filling:
  1. Preheat the oven to 160C, 140C Fan (Gas mark 3)
  2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large casserole dish
  3. Add the beef in batches, browning all sides, then set aside to rest
  4. Add the onions and carrots, 1 tbsp oil and cook on low till coloured
  5. Scatter flour, stirring until it turns brown (ensure the heat is still low, otherwise this will burn the flour)
  6. Tip the meat and juices back into the dish, add the puree and stir well
  7. Pour in the stock, season (I use Garlic, but free to choose your own) and bring to simmer
  8. Cover with a lid, pop it into the oven for around 2 hours.
    You'll know it's done when the beef is tender
    Note: you can pre-make the filling and store for 3 days in the fridge, or 3 months in the freezer!

The pie:
  1. Preheat the oven to 220C, 200 Fan (Gas mark 7)
  2. Pour the filling into a 24-26cm rimmed pie dish and brush the rim with the egg yolk
  3. Unravel the pastry, drape over the edges and use a knife to trim to shape.
    Use the same knife to press the edges into the dish, creating a seal
  4. Optional: Reroll the trimmings into decorations for the top (leaves look great!)
  5. Brush the entire crust with the egg mixture, evenly for a golden colour
  6. Make a few small slits in the centre, bake for 40 minutes until golden
  7. Remove from oven and allow to stand for 3-5 minutes before serving!
